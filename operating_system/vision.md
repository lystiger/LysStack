# LysStack Vision

## Why This Exists

LysStack exists because I do not want to spend my life repeatedly rebuilding knowledge.

Every project, every mistake, every architecture decision, every lesson learned should compound instead of being forgotten.

The purpose of this system is to become an external brain, an engineering operating system, and eventually a trusted partner that grows alongside me.

It is not built to replace my thinking.

It is built to amplify it.

---

# Who I Am

I am an engineer who stands between software, artificial intelligence, and the physical world.

I enjoy building systems that can be used by real people rather than creating technology that exists only inside research papers.

I am interested in:

* Artificial Intelligence
* Computer Vision
* Embedded Systems
* Industrial Automation
* Data Systems
* Human Computer Interaction
* Business Transformation through Technology

I value practical impact over prestige.

I prefer working systems over theoretical perfection.

I believe technology should solve real problems.

# Long-Term Goal

My long-term objective is to become a technology leader capable of building and operating organizations that combine:

* Manufacturing
* Software
* Artificial Intelligence
* Automation

I intend to gain experience internationally, particularly in Japan, where I can learn world-class engineering culture, system design, and industrial practices.

After gaining sufficient experience, I intend to return and help transform my family's businesses into modern technology-enabled organizations.

The goal is not merely to inherit companies.

The goal is to elevate them.

Success is not measured by salary alone.

Success means:

* Building products that survive.
* Creating systems that continue generating value.
* Developing engineering excellence.

# Core Principles

## 1. Build Things That Survive

Temporary solutions may be necessary.

Permanent solutions are the target.

Every project should be designed so that it can evolve rather than be rewritten from scratch.

---

## 2. AI Assists, Humans Decide

Artificial intelligence is a tool.

Responsibility remains human.

AI may generate options.

Humans make decisions.

---

## 3. Practicality Over Hype

I will prioritize technologies that solve real problems.

I will not adopt technology simply because it is fashionable.

---

## 4. Learn By Building

Knowledge becomes valuable only after implementation.

Projects are the primary vehicle for growth.

---

## 5. Systems Thinking

Every problem exists inside a larger system.

Before optimizing a component, understand the system.

---

## 6. Documentation Is An Asset

Code can be rewritten.

Knowledge lost is expensive.

Important decisions must be recorded.

---

## 7. Compounding Matters

Small improvements repeated consistently outperform occasional bursts of effort.

The goal is continuous progress.
